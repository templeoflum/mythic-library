# Epic of Gilgamesh - Source Guide

## Overview
- **Origin**: Mesopotamia (Sumerian/Akkadian)
- **Date**: c. 2100-1200 BCE
- **Type**: Epic poetry
- **Language**: Originally Akkadian, based on earlier Sumerian poems

## Recommended Downloads

### 1. R. Campbell Thompson (1928) - SCHOLARLY
**URL**: https://archive.org/details/thompson-1928-gilgamesh
- **Format**: PDF (59 pages)
- **Quality**: Scholarly literal translation from cuneiform
- **Translator**: R. Campbell Thompson, Oxford Assyriologist
- **Notes**: Baseline scholarly translation, literal rendering from British Museum tablets
- **Download**: Click "PDF" on the right side of the page

### 2. Maureen Gallery Kovacs - SCHOLARLY
**URL**: https://archive.org/details/epic-of-gilgamesh
- **Format**: PDF
- **Quality**: Modern scholarly translation
- **Notes**: Widely used academic translation with clear prose

### 3. A.R. George Translation - SCHOLARLY
**URL**: https://archive.org/details/epicofgilgameshb0000unse
- **Format**: PDF
- **Quality**: Definitive modern scholarly edition
- **Notes**: Includes Akkadian and Sumerian texts, published by Penguin/Barnes & Noble

### 4. Ishtar and Izdubar (1884) - FULLY PUBLIC DOMAIN
**URL**: https://sacred-texts.com/ane/iai/index.htm
- **Format**: HTML
- **Quality**: Victorian poetic adaptation
- **Translator**: Leonidas Le Cenci Hamilton
- **Notes**: Earliest complete English version, "Izdubar" was early reading of Gilgamesh ideograph
- **Historical value**: Shows how the epic was understood in the 19th century

## Structure
The epic consists of 12 tablets:
1. Tablet I: Introduction of Gilgamesh and Enkidu
2. Tablet II: Enkidu's civilization
3. Tablet III: Preparation for Cedar Forest journey
4. Tablet IV: Journey to Cedar Forest
5. Tablet V: Battle with Humbaba
6. Tablet VI: Ishtar's proposal, Bull of Heaven
7. Tablet VII: Enkidu's death
8. Tablet VIII: Gilgamesh mourns Enkidu
9. Tablet IX: Journey to Utnapishtim
10. Tablet X: Meeting with Siduri and ferryman
11. Tablet XI: Flood story and plant of youth
12. Tablet XII: Enkidu's spirit (possibly later addition)

After downloading, verify the text contains:
