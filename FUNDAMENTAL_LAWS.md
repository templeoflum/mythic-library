# Fundamental Laws for ACP Architecture

## Purpose

This document maps fundamental laws from physics, chemistry, biology, mathematics, and information theory to their archetypal equivalents. The ACP system should be grounded in these universal principles so that emergent patterns reflect actual structural relationships found in nature.

## 1. PHYSICS

### 1.1 Conservation Laws

| Physical Law | Statement | Archetypal Equivalent |
|--------------|-----------|----------------------|
| **Conservation of Energy** | Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed | Archetypal energy is conserved - when one archetype weakens, another strengthens. Repression doesn't destroy, it displaces. |
| **Conservation of Momentum** | Momentum is preserved in isolated systems | Transformation paths have inertia - once moving in a direction, archetypes resist reversal |
| **Conservation of Charge** | Net charge is constant | Polarity balance - for every light expression, a shadow potential exists |

### 1.2 Thermodynamics

| Physical Law | Statement | Archetypal Equivalent |
|--------------|-----------|----------------------|
| **First Law** | Energy is conserved | Same as above |
| **Second Law (Entropy)** | Systems tend toward disorder | Undifferentiated systems tend toward polarization; over-polarized systems tend toward integration (cyclic) |
| **Third Law** | Perfect order at absolute zero | The theoretical "poles" (0.0 or 1.0) are asymptotic limits - no real archetype fully occupies them |
| **Zeroth Law** | Thermal equilibrium is transitive | If A resonates with B, and B resonates with C, then A has some resonance with C |

## 6. EMERGENT PRINCIPLES FOR ACP

Based on the above mapping, the following principles should govern ACP:

### 6.1 Conservation Principles
1. **Archetypal Conservation**: Total archetypal energy in a system is conserved
2. **Polarity Conservation**: Light and shadow exist in equal potential
3. **Resonance Transitivity**: If A↔B and B↔C, then A has connection to C

### 6.2 Transformation Principles
4. **Activation Threshold**: Transformations require critical events, not gradual drift
5. **Path Dependence**: History of transformation affects current state
6. **Inertia**: Archetypal states resist change; momentum continues direction

### 6.3 Relationship Principles
7. **Complementary Attraction**: Opposites attract to complete wholeness
8. **Resonance Clustering**: Similar archetypes cluster in spectral space
9. **Niche Differentiation**: Archetypes differentiate to avoid overlap
10. **Shadow Gravity**: Every archetype has pull toward its shadow under stress

### 6.4 Structure Principles
11. **Hierarchical Instantiation**: Primordials → Cultural → Specific (like DNA → Protein → Trait)
12. **Convergent Expression**: Different cultures produce similar patterns (cultural echo)
13. **Contextual Collapse**: Multiple potentials resolve to single expression in specific context
14. **Asymptotic Limits**: Pure poles are theoretical limits, never fully occupied

### 6.5 System Principles
15. **Equilibrium Seeking**: Systems tend toward balanced configuration
16. **Cyclical Dynamics**: Differentiation and integration alternate eternally
17. **Emergent Complexity**: Simple primordial combinations produce complex expressions
18. **Self-Similarity**: Patterns repeat at different scales (fractal-like)
